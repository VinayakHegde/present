🎟️ AI Adoption

Scaling engineering effectiveness

The story

As AI tooling matured, I saw an opportunity to improve engineering productivity while maintaining quality.

Rather than simply encouraging people to use AI tools, I focused on creating a structured process.

We introduced specification-driven delivery where engineers started with PRDs and SDDs before implementation.

AI was used to accelerate repetitive tasks such as scaffolding, testing, documentation and implementation support.

Architecture, review, security and production decisions always remained engineer-owned.

The result was faster delivery, more consistent implementations and better knowledge sharing across teams.

The biggest lesson was that AI creates the most value when paired with strong engineering discipline. It should not replace that discipline.

Full STAR version (local — not in Notion)

Situation: Our company wanted to use AI to improve engineering productivity. There was excitement, but also concerns about quality and maintainability.

Task: Introduce AI without reducing quality.

Action: I helped create a process where Product and Engineering wrote the PRD together. We added technical requirements before asking AI to generate code. The AI created a pull request. The engineer reviewed the code. Product reviewed the behaviour. If something wasn't right, we improved the PRD instead of trying to patch the generated code. We also created shared rules so AI followed the same standards every time.

Result: Our delivery speed improved significantly. Product saw working features earlier. Engineers still owned the final decisions.

Learning: AI works best when it has good instructions. Good engineering is becoming more about designing the process than writing every line of code.
