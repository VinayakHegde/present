🏗️ Platform Rebuild

Architecture and leadership at scale — local story, not yet in Notion

The story — STAR

Situation

When I joined Lantern, part of the product had been built by an external agency.

The product worked, but every new feature introduced regressions. Different teams were blocking each other because everything was tightly connected.

Task

My goal was to build a better foundation. Multiple teams should be able to work independently, without slowing each other down.

Action

I first spent time understanding the product and how every team worked.

I didn't immediately suggest a new framework.

I worked with engineering leadership, backend engineers and Product to understand what was causing the problems.

We agreed to split the system into smaller frontend applications with clear responsibilities.

We also agreed API contracts early so frontend and backend teams could work in parallel.

Instead of rebuilding everything, we migrated one area at a time.

At the same time, I introduced shared standards and documentation so every team followed the same approach.

Result

Engineering grew from one frontend engineer to around twenty engineers across four teams.

Teams could deploy independently.

Frontend could prototype before backend was finished.

The number of integration issues reduced significantly.

I also became the person other teams came to when they had frontend architecture questions.

Learning

Architecture is not about choosing technology. It's about helping teams move faster together.
