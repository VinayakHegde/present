How to Prepare

Preparation playbook — what interviewers are testing, how to structure your intro, how to use your stories, questions to ask, and the day-of routine. The answers themselves live in Common Questions and the Story Bank.

What interviewers are testing

Every answer should reinforce one of these strengths:

  • Product engineer — owns outcomes, not tickets; measured impact (~65% adoption in 5 weeks; reporting 4–6h → ~20min; ~30% support-ticket drop)
  • Ownership — PRD to production, deploy and on-call included; zero P1 at GA
  • Architecture mindset — boundaries, validation, observability; contract-first APIs
  • Communicator — scope docs, ADRs, stakeholder alignment across product/engineering/finance
  • AI-forward with guardrails — daily Cursor/Claude Code, spec-driven, human-owned rules
  • Team multiplier — mentoring, test templates, patterns the squad reuses
  • Startup-compatible — fast delivery, ambiguity, reversible decisions, customer learning

How to structure "tell me about yourself"

Memorise the structure, not the words. The scripted versions — one-line pitch, 90-second arc, 60-second short — live in Common Questions.

  • Structure: who you are → recent impact → how you work → why them
  • Tone: conversational · confident · not a CV recitation
  • Pause after the recent-impact story — invite follow-ups
  • Mention AI naturally inside "how you work", not as a separate paragraph
  • End with the company hook so "why us?" doesn't repeat verbatim later

Using your stories

  • Stories are kept in STAR shape — Situation, Task, Action, Result — each with a "use when asked about…" tag
  • Keep each under 90 seconds spoken. Same platform, different lenses — pick the angle they ask for. Have deeper detail ready for follow-ups
  • Pick 2–3 stories per interview: one primary plus backups matched to the role

Where everything lives

  • Story Bank — seven Notion-synced stories, the competency mapping, and local additional stories
  • Common Questions — the full Q&A bank: intro scripts, motivation & fit, AI & ways of working, quick answers

Questions to ask interviewers

Pick 2–3 based on conversation flow — reference their earlier answers. Prepared questions signal seniority; save comp/logistics for the appropriate stage.

Role (pick 1–2)

  • What does success look like in the first 3–6 months for this role?
  • What are the biggest technical challenges you expect this hire to solve?
  • What is the day-to-day balance of the role in practice (e.g. frontend vs backend, build vs operate)?
  • What distinguishes your strongest engineers?

Team & engineering (pick 1–2)

  • What are the biggest engineering challenges facing the team right now?
  • What does the team own in the next six months — new verticals, new workflows, or platform scaling?
  • How is AI currently used across engineering — tools, workflows, guardrails?
  • What does the paved road look like — from PR to preview environment to production?
  • How do engineers partner with domain experts day-to-day?
  • What does mentoring and code review look like for senior engineers here?
  • How are squads structured — PM, design, data roles?
  • Where are the hardest scaling bottlenecks — ingestion, search, or serving?

Company & culture (pick 1)

  • How much ownership do engineers have over product decisions?
  • How do teams balance experimentation and reliability — especially where trust or compliance is on the line?
  • How does the company organise ownership between product, engineering and design?
  • How do you measure the success of what engineering ships?

Comp & logistics (only if the stage is right)

  • What's the compensation band for this level — base vs total?
  • How does equity work — options, scheme, vesting schedule?
  • Hybrid expectations — days in office?
  • Notice period / start timeline

Day-of checklist — 30 minutes before

  • Rehearse the 90-second intro once, out loud (Common Questions)
  • Re-read the "why this company" answer with the three slots filled for today's company
  • Primary story ready: calculated metrics (2-minute version) — plus 2 backups matched to the role (AI adoption for AI-flavoured roles; legacy migration / two-tier trade-off for platform roles)
  • Measure-success list and AI guardrails one-liner fresh
  • Pick 3 questions from "Questions to ask" — matched to the interviewer's role
  • Verify impact numbers in case they probe (~65% adoption, ~30% ticket drop, 4–6h → ~20min, zero P1)
  • Test camera/mic if remote; water, quiet space, charger; this page open for prep breaks

Pitfalls — what NOT to do

  • Don't spend 15 minutes on framework preferences or library debates (React vs Vue, Jest vs Vitest…)
  • Don't recite API minutiae or lecture on LLM/transformer internals
  • Don't claim AI wrote your production rules or validation logic
  • Don't propose "connect everything to the agent" without governance
  • Don't answer in theory — anchor every answer to something you shipped or would ship in week one
  • Don't pretend deep domain expertise you don't have — use "professional finance audience" and "auditable metrics" instead
  • Don't bad-mouth your current employer, or incumbents in the target industry
  • Don't mention comp unless they raise it

Success criteria — what the interviewer should believe afterwards

  • ✅ Strong senior product engineer — owns outcomes, not tickets
  • ✅ Ownership — PRD to production, on-call included
  • ✅ Good communicator — concise, structured; scope docs, ADRs, stakeholder alignment
  • ✅ Architecture mindset — boundaries, validation, observability
  • ✅ AI-forward with guardrails — practical builder, uses AI with discipline
  • ✅ Can mentor and multiply the team
  • ✅ Helps the team move faster without sacrificing trust — worth progressing

If the interview runs short, make sure AI + ownership + product landed. If it runs long, don't repeat — deepen with examples. Close warmly: thank them, express genuine interest in the mission, ask your 2–3 questions.
