1 · Opening

WHO → NOW → CRAFT → WANT  ·  stop cleanly — let them pick the follow-up

  • Who: product-minded technical lead, ~16 years — React/TS surface, enough backend/architecture to own full delivery
  • Now: Lantern, B2B finance data — shape specs · FE + integration boundaries · partner with BE on APIs & validation
  • Craft: spec-driven, AI-assisted under my review — one clause, no more
  • Want: own outcomes · move fast in ambiguity · use AI seriously
  • Held back: calculated metrics proof point — deploy when they ask for an example

3 · How I work — one-liners

  • With PMs: co-author the PRD — alternatives, not nos
  • Quality: built into the workflow, not a final gate — good practice improves speed and quality
  • Priorities: impact → urgency → risk → dependencies → reversibility → effort; ranking visible and shared
  • Incomplete info: reversible decisions fast; irreversible ones get the riskiest assumption validated first
  • Saying no: constraint + risk + an alternative that still serves the outcome

4 · The five stories — in this order

1🔍 Financial Reporting Discovery — found the real problem behind the stated request. Customer focus · ambiguity · Product · changing direction
2🏗️ Platform Rebuild — agency-built product → platform for four teams (1 → ~20 engineers). Hardest problem · architecture · org scaling · influence
3🤝 Contract-First Collaboration — API contracts removed FE/BE blocking. Cross-team · dependencies · parallel delivery
4🎟️ AI Adoption — AI-assisted development with guardrails. Change management · sceptics · productivity — supporting evidence, don't lead with it
5🖋️ Mentorship — scaling through others. Developing engineers · leadership

Open with story 1 when possible — an early customer-focused win that's easy to follow. Save Platform Rebuild for the hardest question.

6 · Self-awareness arc

MISTAKE (rushed to code) → FEEDBACK (too long to the point → answer first) → WEAKNESS (over-corrected → now time-boxed)  ·  one arc, not three flaws

  • Mistake: "jumped into implementation too quickly — solved the requirement they described, not the problem they had" → optimise for understanding the problem first
  • Feedback: "answers took too long to reach the point → answer first, pause, add context — later feedback praised communication"
  • Weakness: "rushed early, over-corrected into over-exploring — today I time-box discovery and validate the riskiest assumption first"

Mistake story still needs real grounding (feature, user, cost) — checklist here.

7 · Closing

Why should we hire you — summary close"You need someone who can own the frontend and full-stack direction. You need someone who works closely with Product and helps several teams deliver consistently. My background combines exactly those three things: hands-on technical depth, product thinking, and experience creating standards. Those standards help other engineers move faster."

Your questions — pick 2–3 by audience

  • Engineers: "What does idea → production look like here — who specs, who reviews, who deploys?"
  • Manager: "What would a great first six months look like — what would you want shipped or changed?"
  • Product/TPM: "How do Product and Engineering resolve scope disagreements here?"
  • + one company-specific question from research the night before
